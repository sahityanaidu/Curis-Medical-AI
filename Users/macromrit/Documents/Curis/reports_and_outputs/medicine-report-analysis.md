# Medical Stock Report

## Burnol Cream
### Description
Burnol Cream contains Aminacrine and Cetrimide. It is primarily used as an antiseptic for treating minor burns. It provides immediate relief, prevents infection, and aids in quick healing. It can also be used for minor wounds, cuts, scrapes, abrasions, and insect bites.

### Composition
- **Aminacrine**: An antiseptic component that helps prevent infections.
- **Cetrimide**: Another antiseptic that aids in quick healing.

### Uses
Prescribed for minor burns, minor wounds, cuts, and abrasions.

### Side Effects
May cause itching, irritation, redness, or a burning sensation at the application site. These are generally mild but consult a doctor if they persist or worsen.

## Betadine
### Description
Betadine contains Povidone-iodine, which is a broad-spectrum antiseptic for topical application. It is used to treat or prevent skin infections in minor cuts, scrapes, burns, and can be used in medical settings to help prevent infection and promote healing in skin wounds, pressure sores, or surgical incisions.

### Composition
- **Povidone-iodine**: A broad-spectrum antiseptic that reduces microbial presence and prevents infection.

### Uses
Used on the skin to treat or prevent infections and to help in the healing of minor cuts, scrapes, burns, and wounds. It can also be used inside the mouth for temporary relief of minor mouth pain or irritation, sore throat, or canker sore pain.

### Side Effects
Possible side effects include swelling, pain, warmth, redness, oozing, or other signs of infection; blistering or crusting; or severe irritation, itching, or burning.

Both medications are topically applied and have antiseptic properties, making them essential in first aid situations involving minor injuries. They should be used as directed by a healthcare professional to avoid any potential adverse effects.
